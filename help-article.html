The classification of most dangerous goods is carried out by shippers (manufacturers) of dangerous goods. To do this, they can contact the competent authorities.
For example, according to the German Framework Regulation (GGVSE), the Federal Office for Materials Research and Testing (BAM) is responsible for the classification and allocation of most hazardous goods (hazardous waste).

Danger signs

To indicate the dangerous properties of dangerous goods, danger signs are used (see pictures relating to individual classes of dangerous goods), which must be applied to dangerous goods or packaging with such goods. The danger label system is based on the classification of dangerous goods and is designed for the following purposes:

make dangerous goods easily recognizable from a distance by the general appearance of the danger signs on them (symbol, color and shape);
provide, by means of the color of the hazard labels, the first useful indication regarding loading and unloading operations, cargo stowage and cargo separation.

The identification of dangerous goods by UN number is carried out by the Dangerous Goods List in Table A of Chapter 3.2 of ADR. In this list all dangerous goods are listed in the order of the UN numbers assigned to them.

If the UN number in the Dangerous Goods List is missing, the identification of the goods is incorrect.

For dangerous goods not specified by name in the Dangerous Goods List, the UN number shall be determined after determining the class, type of subsidiary risk (if any) and packing group (if necessary).
